# Resume Writing Tips

A resume's job is to get you an interview. It should be easy to scan in under a
minute and show clear evidence of what you can do.

## Structure
- **One page** for students and early-career candidates.
- Order: contact details, short summary, skills, experience/projects, education.
- Put the most relevant section highest for the role you are applying to.

## Writing strong bullet points
- Start each bullet with an action verb (Built, Analysed, Automated, Led).
- Show a result or number where you can ("cut report time from 2 hours to 10 minutes").
- Avoid vague lines like "responsible for various tasks".

## Skills section
- List the specific tools and languages the job asks for, only if you actually
  know them.
- Group them (Languages, Tools, Databases) so a reader finds them fast.

## Common mistakes
- A long paragraph objective instead of a short summary.
- Listing every course or skill instead of the relevant ones.
- Spelling and formatting errors — proofread, and keep fonts and spacing consistent.

## Tailoring for a specific job
Read the job description, note the skills it repeats, and make sure those exact
words appear on your resume if they are true for you. This also helps you pass the
automated filters many companies use.
